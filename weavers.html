The Hands Behind the Cloth

Meet the Weavers

আমাদের তাঁতিরা — যারা স্বপ্ন বোনেন সুতোয়

These are not anonymous artisans. They are mothers, daughters, teachers, and dreamers — women whose hands have spent decades learning a language that cannot be written, only woven. Every piece you wear carries the story of one of them.

"আমরা বিক্রি করি না শুধু কাপড়। আমরা বহন করি সেই নারীর স্বপ্ন, যে ভোরবেলা তাঁতে বসেন — শুধু এইটুকু বিশ্বাস নিয়ে যে তাঁর শিল্পের মূল্য আছে।"

"We do not merely sell fabric. We carry the dreams of the woman who sits at the loom at dawn — with only this one belief: that her craft has worth."

Jamdani · 28 years
28
সরস্বতী দেবী
Murshidabad, West Bengal

Saraswati learned jamdani at age 9, sitting beside her mother's loom in Murshidabad. She married into a weaving family and raised three daughters, all of whom now weave for বুনন. Her signature motif — a three-petal lotus — appears in our most beloved bridal sarees. "The loom is my language," she says. "I say things with thread that I cannot say with words."

Kantha · 35 years
35
রাধারানী মণ্ডল
Bolpur, Birbhum

Radharani is a kantha legend in Bolpur. Her running stitch is so precise that art historians have documented her work as a rare surviving example of pre-industrial kantha technique. After losing her husband early, kantha became her livelihood and her meditation. She now trains 40 younger women in her village, ensuring the stitch survives another generation.

Block Print · 20 years
20
সুমিত্রা হাঁসদা
Purulia, West Bengal

Sumitra is a Santali woman whose block printing draws from her tribal heritage — geometric forms, animals of the forest, harvest symbols passed down through oral tradition. She carves her own printing blocks from wood she collects from the forest. বুনন was the first platform that paid her fairly for her art. She used her first payment to put her son through engineering school.

Natural Dye · 15 years
15
মীরা মাহাতো
Jharkhand border, Bengal

Meera doesn't weave — she colours. A master of natural dyeing, she extracts pigments from indigo plants, pomegranate rind, turmeric, and forest bark. Her colours don't fade; they age. She supplies the dyed yarns that give বুনন's cotton pieces their distinctive, earthy richness.

Tant · 40 years
40
কমলা বসাক
Dhaniakhali, Hooghly

Kamala is 68 years old and has been at the tant loom since she was 28. Her family has woven tant for four generations in Dhaniakhali — the village that gave Bengal its most beloved everyday saree. She weaves 3 sarees a day, without counting. "The loom doesn't need instructions," she says. "It knows what to do. So do I."

Zari · 18 years
18
পূর্ণিমা সাহা
Shantiniketan, Birbhum

Purnima studied under a Tagore-era weaving tradition at Shantiniketan's craft school before joining বুনন. She specializes in zari borders — pure gold thread work woven directly into fabric, not stitched on later. Her bridal dupatta borders are sought after across Bengal. She is also our youngest master weaver, at 34.

Why It Matters

আমরা কেন করি এই কাজ

Fair Wages

Every weaver receives 40% above market rate. We believe the price of craft must reflect the time, skill and soul invested in it.

Skill Preservation

বুনন runs free workshops where master weavers teach their techniques to the next generation, ensuring no art form is lost.

Sustainable Craft

We use natural dyes, organic fibres, and traditional methods that are gentle on the earth — the same earth our weavers live on.

Women First

92% of our weavers are women. Financial independence through craft has enabled education, healthcare, and dignity for hundreds of families.
